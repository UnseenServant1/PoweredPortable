Jackery vs. EcoFlow: Which Brand Makes the Better Power Station in 2026?

Updated March 2026 · 12 min read

Jackery and EcoFlow are the two biggest names in portable power stations. If you've spent more than five minutes shopping for one, you've seen both brands dominating the search results, the Amazon listings, and every "best of" roundup out there. But they take meaningfully different approaches to product design, pricing, and feature sets.

This guide breaks down how the two brands actually compare across the things that matter most: build quality, battery performance, charging speed, solar compatibility, app experience, and value for money. By the end, you'll know which one is the better fit for how you plan to use it.

The Quick Verdict

Choose Jackery if you prioritize simplicity, reliability, and a proven track record. Jackery units are straightforward to use, well-built, and tend to offer strong value as prices drop. Their solar panels are among the best in the business.

Choose EcoFlow if you want cutting-edge features, faster charging, and a more connected experience. EcoFlow pushes harder on innovation with features like UPS switchover, a polished app, and modular battery expansion. They typically launch new tech first.

Brand Overview

Jackery

Founded in 2012, Jackery was one of the earliest companies to bring lithium portable power stations to the consumer market. They've built a reputation for rugged, dependable products with an orange-and-black design language that's become iconic in the space. Their lineup spans from small 240 Wh units up to multi-thousand watt-hour systems. Jackery tends to focus on doing fewer things very well rather than loading up features.

EcoFlow

EcoFlow launched in 2017 and quickly became Jackery's main competitor by leaning into technology and innovation. Their product line is broader, with everything from the ultralight River series (under 8 lbs) to the massive Delta Pro 3 for whole-home backup. EcoFlow was one of the first brands to offer app connectivity, rapid wall charging, and UPS-grade power switchover in a portable unit.

Head-to-Head Comparison

Build Quality & Design

Both brands make well-built products, but the design philosophy differs. Jackery units feel solid and no-nonsense. The build is rugged and practical, with chunky handles and clear, simple controls. They have the look and feel of equipment built for outdoor use, which is exactly what they are.

EcoFlow products feel more like consumer electronics. The fit and finish is slightly more refined, with cleaner lines, higher-resolution displays, and a more modern aesthetic. Their larger units include thoughtful touches like telescoping handles and built-in wheels. Neither brand has widespread issues with build quality, so this comes down to personal preference.

Edge: EcoFlow for design and polish. Jackery for rugged simplicity.

Battery & Performance

Both brands have transitioned their main lineups to LiFePO4 batteries, which means you're getting 3,000+ cycle life regardless of which one you pick. In terms of raw efficiency (how much of the stated capacity you actually get to use), the two brands trade blows depending on the specific model and load type.

EcoFlow units tend to have a slight advantage in real-world efficiency with low-wattage, sustained loads (like running a laptop or LED lighting). Jackery units perform comparably or slightly better with higher-draw appliances like mini fridges and power tools. The difference is small enough that it shouldn't be the deciding factor for most buyers.

Where EcoFlow pulls ahead is the UPS feature on their Delta line. The 10-millisecond switchover means your devices stay powered even during an instant grid failure, which is a significant advantage for home backup use.

Edge: EcoFlow, primarily for the UPS feature.

Charging Speed

This is one of EcoFlow's biggest strengths. Their wall charging speeds are consistently among the fastest in the industry. The Delta 3 Plus, for example, goes from 0 to 80% in under an hour via AC input. Jackery has improved their charging speeds significantly with recent models (the Explorer 2000 v2 is much faster than the original), but EcoFlow is still the leader here.

Solar charging is a closer race. Both brands support high solar input wattages on their larger models. Jackery's solar panels have a slight reputation advantage for durability and consistent output, while some users have reported EcoFlow's foldable panels can warp slightly after extended storage. On the power station side, both brands handle solar input well.

Edge: EcoFlow for wall charging. Jackery for solar panel quality.

App Experience

Both brands offer companion apps that connect via Bluetooth and Wi-Fi for monitoring and control. EcoFlow's app is more feature-rich. You can toggle eco mode, set charging limits, update firmware, and monitor real-time input/output data with clean, detailed graphs. Jackery's app covers the basics (battery level, input/output monitoring) but is less polished and has fewer configuration options.

If you're someone who wants to set it and forget it, neither app is required for basic operation. But if you appreciate granular control and a well-designed interface, EcoFlow's app is the better experience.

Edge: EcoFlow, clearly.

Product Range & Expandability

EcoFlow has a broader product range, spanning from the tiny River 3 (245 Wh, under $200) up to the Delta Pro 3 (4,096 Wh, $2,500+). Many of their mid-range and high-end models support expansion batteries, letting you add capacity without buying an entirely new unit. This modular approach is a big plus for people who might want to start small and scale up.

Jackery's lineup is slightly narrower but covers the most common use cases well. Their expansion options are more limited compared to EcoFlow. If you think you'll want to grow your system over time, EcoFlow gives you more flexibility.

Edge: EcoFlow for range and expandability.

Pricing & Value

This is where Jackery fights back. Jackery units have become significantly more affordable in 2026, with aggressive price drops on models like the Explorer 2000 v2 (frequently found under $800 for over 2,000 Wh). Jackery also tends to run more frequent sales and bundle deals that include solar panels.

EcoFlow's pricing reflects the additional features and technology baked into their products. You're paying a premium for the UPS, the app, the faster charging, and the modular ecosystem. Whether that premium is justified depends on how much those features matter to your specific use case.

For budget-conscious buyers, Jackery consistently offers more watt-hours per dollar, especially at the mid-range and high-end tiers.

Edge: Jackery for raw value. EcoFlow if the extra features justify the cost.

This comparison highlights the tradeoff perfectly. For roughly the same price, Jackery gives you double the capacity while EcoFlow gives you UPS, expandability, a better app, and faster charging. Your use case determines which set of advantages matters more.

So Which Should You Buy?

Go with Jackery if: you want maximum capacity for the money, you'll primarily use it for camping and outdoor activities, you prefer simple plug-and-play operation, or you plan to pair it with solar panels for off-grid use. Jackery makes some of the best solar panels in the category, and their units pair seamlessly with them.

Go with EcoFlow if: you want cutting-edge features like UPS and expandability, fast wall charging is important (apartment dwellers or last-minute packers), you want the best app experience, or you plan to build a modular power system that grows with your needs over time.

Either way, you're buying from a reputable brand with solid warranties and good customer support. Both companies make excellent products. The "right" choice depends entirely on what you value most.
